Business Review Dashboard Tool

Tableau Snowflake SQL Excel Figma PowerPoint Canva Azure DevOps

Business Review Dashboard Tool

Context

Previously, the monthly business review was assembled manually using Tableau, Excel, and PowerPoint. This process was time-consuming, error-prone, and took up an entire workweek every month. The dashboards lacked user-friendly design, and data inconsistency between clients made standardization almost impossible.

Goal

Create an automated, scalable, and interactive dashboard platform that could be used both internally by management and externally by clients. The dashboard needed to provide real-time insights at daily, weekly, monthly, and custom period levels.

My Role & Team
  • Researched client needs and relevant KPIs
  • Designed a user-friendly interface in collaboration with stakeholders
  • Built the dashboard with secure access for internal and external users
  • Worked closely with BI, operations, customer service, IT, HR, and account management
Approach & Methodology

1. User Research & Business Analyse

  • Interviews met accountmanagers, operations & klanten
  • Analyse van bestaande KPI’s, dataverschillen per klant en hun wensen
  • Documenteren van use cases & databehoeften

2. UX/UI Design

  • Wireframes gemaakt in PowerPoint, Figma en Canva
  • Aandacht voor hiërarchie, navigatie & informatieflow
  • Verschillende versies getest met stakeholders (intern & extern)

3. Data Engineering & Validatie

  • Verbinden & structureren van databronnen met Snowflake SQL
  • Validatie in Excel t.o.v. eerdere manuele rapporten
  • Oplossen van inconsistenties tussen klanten/types goederen
  • Bepalen van calculation logic (e.g. lead times, fill rate, cut-offs)

4. Dashboardontwikkeling in Tableau

  • Tabbladen gebouwd rond thema’s: Inbound, Returns, Outbound, Inventory, etc.
  • Toevoegen van filters (bv. warehouse, klant, tijd, ordertype)
  • Zoekfunctionaliteit en UX-elementen (legenda, tooltips, kleurcodes)

5. Testing & Oplevering

  • MVP opgeleverd intern & getest door operationele teams
  • Feedbackcycli ingebouwd met BI, accountmanagement en klant
  • Versiebeheer & logbeheer opgezet
  • Rollout naar klanten met training

6. Training & Support

  • Training gegeven aan accountmanagers en interne teams
  • Documentatie & demoslides voorzien
  • Zelf verantwoordelijk voor klantpresentaties in Business Reviews
Results & Impact
  • 30+ hours saved per week through automation
  • Real-time insights accessible to both clients and managers from a single platform
  • Higher client satisfaction thanks to transparency and ease of use
  • Recognized as best practice and scaled to other business teams
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: Tableau, Snowflake SQL, Excel, Figma, PowerPoint, Canva, Azure DevOps
  • Skills: Data engineering, UX/UI design, stakeholder management, Agile project delivery, communication & training

With this project, I bridged the gap between complex data and clear, accessible insights — transforming the business review from a static report into a dynamic and scalable decision-making tool.

HR Analytics Dashboard

HR Analytics Dashboard

Context

Our organization needed better insights into key HR metrics such as turnover, absenteeism, and productivity by team or location. Existing reports were built manually in Excel and PowerPoint, leading to errors and outdated data. Additionally, HR and operational data were spread across different systems, making it hard to get a consistent overview.

Goal

Develop a scalable and reliable HR dashboard that integrates HR and operational data. The tool should help HR, operations, and management make faster, data-driven decisions around workforce planning, turnover, and employee deployment.

My Role & Team
  • Led the data analysis, engineering, and dashboard development from end to end
  • Collaborated closely with HR and BI teams to align on definitions and KPIs

Worked with:

  • HR: defining KPIs and validating concepts (turnover, contracts, absences)
  • BI & IT: integrating operational systems and accessing WMS data
  • Project Management: scoping requirements, timelines, and priorities

Approach & Methodology
  • Agile delivery in SCRUM sprints
  • Held workshops with HR and management to gather reporting needs and business questions
  • Structured data from HR and WMS to ensure consistency
  • Engineered a robust dataset in Snowflake SQL with calculated fields for turnover, absenteeism, contract types, site productivity, etc.
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboards with filters for team, contract type, location, and time period
  • Validated data with HR experts and Excel-based parallel testing
  • Rolled out in production with documentation and training
Results & Impact
  • Fully automated HR reporting process
  • 10+ hours saved per week on manual work for HR
  • Real-time visibility into turnover, absences, and productivity by site
  • Improved collaboration between HR and operations via a shared dashboard
  • Supported strategic decisions on workforce planning and retention
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: Snowflake SQL, Tableau, Excel, Azure DevOps
  • Skills: Data Analytics & Engineering, Business Requirements Gathering, Stakeholder Communication, Project Management (SCRUM), Cross-functional Collaboration

This project enabled HR to make smarter, faster decisions and helped embed a more data-driven approach to workforce management across the organization.

Inbound Operations Tracker

Excel Trello Monday.com WMS Snowflake SQL Tableau Azure DevOps

Inbound Operations Tracker

Context

Tracking inbound logistics flows (incoming goods) was fragmented and manual. Critical information such as status updates, arrival times, box info, issues, and delays were tracked in isolated Excel files. This approach was:

  • Error-prone
  • Not centrally managed
  • Not scalable
  • Unusable for data analysis or reporting

Essential data was also missing from the WMS, making coordination difficult for both floor staff and management.

Goal

Design a central digital workflow to register, track, and analyze inbound operations. It should:

  • Provide real-time visibility of inbound flows
  • Consolidate data from different sources and replace manual steps
  • Be usable by both floor teams and management
  • Support analysis and reporting for process optimization
My Role & Team

I led the project end-to-end with a dual focus on process optimization and data engineering. Key tasks included:

  • Analyzing current Excel workflows
  • Implementing and configuring Trello and Monday.com
  • Designing and deploying an internal web-based data tool with IT
  • Structuring the data for dashboarding

Collaborating with:

  • Warehouse supervisors & team leads: operational needs and testing
  • Operations management: process alignment
  • IT: development of internal data tooling
  • BI team: dashboard design and data integration

Approach & Methodology

The project was phased as follows:

Phase 1 – Excel Optimization:

  • Standardized columns and definitions
  • Automated calculations and validations

Phase 2 – Trello Implementation:

  • Visual tracking via cards and labels
  • Daily usage by team leads
  • Manual data extraction for reporting

Phase 3 – Migration to Monday.com:

  • More structure, automation, and access controls
  • Reporting features added

Phase 4 – Internal Data Tool:

  • Web interface where team leads input WMS-missing data
  • Back-end connection with WMS for a uniform dataset

Phase 5 – Dashboarding in Tableau:

  • Data processed using Snowflake SQL
  • Visualization of inbound volumes, timings, and issues
  • Filterable by customer, warehouse, inbound type, date
Results & Impact
  • 20+ hours/week saved through reduced manual follow-up
  • Real-time insights available to team leads, managers, and even clients
  • Reduced dependency on Excel and visual project tools
  • Better analysis of disruptions and performance bottlenecks
  • Transparent data flows from floor to dashboard
  • Scalable solution now used as a template in other warehouses
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: Excel, Trello, Monday.com, Custom Internal Tool, Snowflake SQL, Tableau, Azure DevOps
  • Skills: Data Analytics & Engineering, Operational Process Analysis, Dashboard Design & Visualization, Change Management & Adoption, Agile & SCRUM, Stakeholder Communication & Training

This tool fundamentally improved how inbound logistics are tracked and optimized, creating value across teams and becoming a model for other operations.

WMS Training & Enablement Program

PowerPoint HTML CSS JavaScript Figma WMS

WMS Training & Enablement Program

Context

The warehouse relies on a complex Warehouse Management System (WMS). Both new hires and experienced staff struggled with system processes, causing:

  • Incorrect data entry or interpretation
  • Misunderstandings around inventory, order statuses, and returns
  • Delays in stock corrections or order flows
  • Communication breakdowns between operations, IT, and BI

A structured and centralized training was urgently needed for all WMS users in operational roles.

Objective

Develop a hands-on training program that:

  • Explains the full WMS logistics flow (from goods receiving to returns and adjustments)
  • Clarifies definitions, codes, statuses, and system events
  • Reduces errors and improves process speed
  • Is interactive and modular for reuse during onboarding or refreshers
Target Audience & My Role

Mandatory training for:

  • Team leads in inbound, outbound, returns, inventory, transit
  • Operational BI staff
  • Shift leaders and floor coordinators

My role:

  • Full WMS process analysis
  • Developing and delivering training (in-person & digital)
  • Building interactive modules and exercises
  • Coordinating with IT, Operations, and HR
Approach & Methodology

Process Mapping & Analysis

  • In-depth review of: receiving, picking, packing, shipping, returns, stock corrections
  • Explanation of status codes, triggers, edge cases
  • Mapping physical actions to digital WMS steps

Training Materials

  • PowerPoint presentations with visuals & flows
  • Self-made animated videos explaining process flows
  • Interactive exercises with quizzes, drag & drop, matching
  • Documentation with key terms, formulas, and exceptions

Digital Learning Platform

  • Custom-built training site using HTML/CSS/JS
  • Modules per process, repeatable for onboarding
  • Progress tracking per participant

Training Delivery

  • Small group sessions
  • Role-specific tailoring (e.g., extra BI content for analysts)
  • Real case examples and common mistakes
  • Participant evaluations
Results & Impact
  • 100% participation from all required operational roles
  • Noticeable drop in stock correction and return flow errors
  • Improved understanding of BI data generation from WMS
  • Training became a standard part of onboarding
  • Program still used as reference and troubleshooting guide
  • Replicated in other warehouses in the group
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: PowerPoint, HTML/CSS/JS, Canva, Figma, Excel
  • Skills: Train-the-Trainer Certified, Didactic Process Translation, Storytelling, Interactive E-Learning Design, Stakeholder Alignment (Ops, HR, IT)

This program created a lasting improvement in how warehouse staff understand and work with the WMS, reducing errors and enhancing cross-department collaboration.

Xoombike – Smart Mobility UX Design

Context

In many urban and suburban areas, parents face stressful morning routines trying to get their kids to school on time. Cities, meanwhile, are seeking sustainable alternatives to traditional school transport. Xoombike addresses both:

  • A battery-powered bike train that safely transports children to school
  • Equipped with modular carts, track & trace, and a mobile planning tool
  • Designed for kids, parents, schools, and municipalities
Objective
  • Create a user-friendly, scalable and safe school transport solution
  • Focus on smart design: lightweight, modular, child-friendly
  • UX-first software: live location, calendar planning, pick-up points
  • Prioritize sustainability, cost-efficiency and safety
My Role
  • Led the project from concept to prototype
  • Defined mission and target users (parents & schools in suburban areas)
  • Facilitated workshops and co-creation sprints
  • Coordinated the UX/UI design process (user journeys, wireframes, mockups)
  • Managed stakeholders: teachers, parents, volunteers, and local authorities
  • Integrated hardware, software, and user feedback into one unified solution
Approach & Methodology

Agile & Co-Creation

  • Agile delivery using Trello Kanban boards
  • Daily stand-ups via Microsoft Teams
  • Design sprints with testing moments: concept → design → prototype → feedback

Stakeholder Involvement

  • Weekly teacher feedback loops
  • Monthly demo sessions with parents and schools
  • Co-creation with volunteers acting as bike drivers
Development Phases

Strategic Research

  • Market analysis showed e-school buses were expensive and rigid
  • Target audience: parents in smaller cities lacking eco-friendly transport options
  • Functional requirements defined via interviews and surveys

Concept & Design

  • Modular bike train concept: electric go-cart + linkable child wagons
  • UX goals: simplicity, kid-friendly visuals, intuitive planning
  • Wireframes and visuals created in PowerPoint & Canva

Prototyping & Software

  • First Xoombike prototype tested for usability, safety, and flexibility
  • Track & Trace app with features like: Real-time GPS tracking, Built-in calendar & planner, Customizable pick-up points (Uber-style)

Validation & Feedback

  • Test rides with families, kids and schools
  • Safety adjustments added (helmets, reflective vests, stability upgrades)
  • UI improvements: simplified login, clearer routing, smarter notifications
Results & Impact
  • Positive pilot feedback from schools and parents
  • High willingness to use thanks to user-friendly design and safety measures
  • Cost savings through community-driven volunteer drivers
  • Eco-friendly solution promoting mobility, health, and reduced stress
  • Mobile app as key differentiator: parents could live-track and adjust pick-up spots
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: Trello, Canva, PowerPoint, Microsoft Teams, Custom Prototyping Platform
  • Skills: UX Design, Prototyping, User Research, Agile SCRUM, Stakeholder Engagement, Interdisciplinary Collaboration
“Xoombike was more than a bike train – it was a sustainable vision for children's mobility, built on empathy, smart tech, and ease of use.”

Start2Found – Co-Founder Matching Platform

Context

Many young entrepreneurs struggle to find co-founders or team members who share their vision and have complementary skills. At the same time, aspiring starters want to engage with innovative ventures but lack an entry point. Start2Found bridges that gap with a dual-sided SaaS platform where founders and starters can connect through a swipe-based interface—professional in nature, inspired by the success of dating apps.

Objective
  • Build an MVP for a matching platform focused on vision and skill alignment
  • Allow founders to pitch their ideas and needs
  • Enable starters to match based on skill sets and interests
  • Create a mobile-first, intuitive swipe experience
  • UX centered on detailed user personas
My Role
  • Product Manager and Co-designer shaping product vision and UX direction
  • Defined core user personas (founders & starters)
  • Mapped user journeys and flows, created wireframes
  • Designed the platform concept in Figma
  • Pitched the concept at innovation events and competitions
  • Explored SEO/SEA and go-to-market strategies
  • Coordinated Agile sprints using Trello and Microsoft Teams
Approach & Methodology

Agile + User-Centered Design

  • Iterative development with feedback loops and design sprints
  • Stakeholder interviews with both founders and starters
  • Partnered with Gentrepreneur and Expedition DO! for validation

Design Phase

  • Personas crafted for both user groups
  • User flows prioritized ease of use and clarity
  • Swipe-based interface designed in Figma:
    • Founders pitch ideas + desired skill sets
    • Starters create a skill and interest profile
    • Matches occur when both sides swipe right

Validation & Testing

  • Pitched and demoed at Gentrepreneur events
  • Interface and profile features refined based on user feedback
  • Key insight: need for advanced filtering and links with educational institutions
Development & Marketing
  • Visual branding (logo, icon, UI design) in Figma
  • SEO/SEA tactics mapped for early growth
  • Outbound strategy targeting entrepreneurship students
  • Partnership outreach with Arteveldehogeschool and UGent for pilot testing
Results & Impact
  • Functional prototype tested and validated with real users
  • Strong need confirmed within the Flemish startup landscape
  • Positive stakeholder reactions to usability and concept potential
  • Gained insights into how digital platforms can facilitate co-founder discovery
  • Growth strategy in place, though full development was paused due to shifting career priorities
Tools & Skills
  • Tools: Figma, Trello, Microsoft Teams, Canva
  • Skills: UX Design, SaaS Concept Development, Agile Product Management, User Research, Branding & Pitching
“Start2Found proved that matching vision and skills doesn’t have to be left to chance—with the right digital flow, founders and starters can connect at exactly the right moment.”
